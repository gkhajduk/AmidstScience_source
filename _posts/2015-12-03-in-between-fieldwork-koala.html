--- layout: post title: 'In-between fieldwork: Koala' date: '2015-12-03T19:20:00.000Z' author: Amidst Science tags: - in-between fieldwork modified_time: '2016-02-27T19:49:17.189Z' thumbnail: http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-Il1vIQYax_M/Vjz8tOGY4uI/AAAAAAAAAr4/blBF3z1ttGE/s72-c/DSC04275.jpg blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-7745412915677497145.post-3705542269681214925 blogger_orig_url: http://amidstscience.blogspot.com/2015/12/in-between-fieldwork-koala.html --- Koalas in Canberra are extinct, which is a great shame. Tidbinbilla Nature Reserve used to have a few captive koalas, but from what I have been told all but one of them died in the big 2003 bush fires. Poor things.

Some koalas have been translocated back to the reserve recently and I saw those. They have a little house there, where some of them sleep. They live up to the cuteness standards.




I was also lucky enough to see one of them awake and climbing around. At one point he was hanging using only his arms, flailing his legs around - pretty funny sight!
