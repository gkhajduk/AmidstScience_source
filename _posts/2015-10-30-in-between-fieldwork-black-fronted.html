--- layout: post title: 'In-between fieldwork: Black-fronted dotterel' date: '2015-10-30T18:06:00.000Z' author: Amidst Science tags: - in-between fieldwork modified_time: '2016-02-27T19:49:17.209Z' thumbnail: http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-lugEfspt3Wk/Vjzt82or5NI/AAAAAAAAAqo/evl4Z8mgKhk/s72-c/DSC04892.jpg blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-7745412915677497145.post-6312156436722513132 blogger_orig_url: http://amidstscience.blogspot.com/2015/10/in-between-fieldwork-black-fronted.html --- Black-fronted dotterels (Elseyornis melanops) are not very common around Canberra and I only managed to see a pair during my visit.

They were very easy too spook and didn't allow me to approach at all, every time I tried to take a step in their direction they would strut off hurriedly. They were moving around quite nervously and most of my photos came out rather blurry.
