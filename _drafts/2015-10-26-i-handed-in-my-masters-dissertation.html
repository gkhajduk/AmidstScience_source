I watched a few random things including an episode of  The Big Bang Theory in which Amy feels excluded and uses the line "one of those things is not like the others"*.

It made me think. I love research, I want to do a PhD and stay in academia. My closest family has always been really supportive. But. I'm the first one to make it to university in my family. More than that, I don't want to have a normal job like a normal person should. I know my parents are proud of me, but they haven't been able to help me navigate college and university, or any other aspect of my life abroad (and I moved away age 16). I find it sad that I can't fully share with them what I'm doing, why I'm doing it and why I love it. The fact that I'm currently working on sperm doesn't help the matter. I think that they are happy for me, but when I call excited about an aspect of my project the topic always gets changed straight away. To strawberries, or to someone who I used to know getting married.


all my friends from middle and high school are getting married, settled and having babies. Some of them live with their parents or have houses handed down to them.
